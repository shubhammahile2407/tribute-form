THE RUN MACHINE VIRAT KOHLI

--- A man who inspires billions ---

Virat Kohli, widely regarded as one of the greatest cricketers of all time, is an Indian international cricketer. Born on November 5, 1988, in Delhi, India, Kohli's journey to cricketing stardom began at a young age. Known for his unwavering dedication, passion, and exceptional talent, Kohli has left an indelible mark on the cricketing world. Making his debut for the Indian national team in 2008, Kohli quickly established himself as a prolific batsman, with a remarkable ability to chase down targets under pressure. His aggressive yet technically sound batting style has earned him admiration from fans and fellow cricketers alike.

Following are few points telling kohli's life story in short:

  • Life and Cricketing Beginnings: Virat Kohli was born on November 5, 1988, in Delhi, India, to Prem Kohli and Saroj Kohli. He grew up in Uttam Nagar and started playing cricket at a very young age.
    Kohli's father supported his cricketing aspirations and enrolled him in the West Delhi Cricket Academy when he was just 9 years old. Rajkumar Sharma, his first coach, recognized his talent early on and played a crucial role in his development.
  • Domestic Career: Kohli rose through the ranks in Delhi cricket and captained the Under-19 team to victory in the 2008 ICC Under-19 Cricket World Cup.
    He made his first-class debut for Delhi in 2006 and performed consistently well in domestic cricket, earning him a spot in the Indian national team.
  • International Debut and Early Success: Kohli made his international debut for India in August 2008 during an ODI against Sri Lanka. He scored his first century against Sri Lanka in 2009, solidifying his place in the team.
    His Test debut came in June 2011 against the West Indies, where he scored a century in the first innings.
  • Rise to Stardom: Kohli's aggressive yet technically sound batting style quickly made him a fan favorite. He became known for his ability to chase down targets and perform under pressure.
    His breakthrough year came in 2012 when he scored five centuries in ODIs, establishing himself as one of the best batsmen in the world.
  • Captaincy and Leadership: Kohli was appointed as the captain of the Indian Test team in 2014, succeeding MS Dhoni. He later took over the captaincy in limited-overs formats as well.
    Under his leadership, India achieved significant success, including Test series wins in Australia and South Africa.
  • Captaincy and Leadership: Kohli holds numerous records in international cricket, including the fastest batsman to reach 8,000, 9,000, 10,000, and 11,000 runs in ODIs.
    He has received several awards and accolades, including the Garfield Sobers Trophy for ICC Cricketer of the Year multiple times.
  • Off the Field: Kohli is involved in various philanthropic activities and has supported causes related to education, health, and underprivileged children.
    He is also a successful entrepreneur, with investments in fashion, fitness, and hospitality sectors.
  • Personal Life: Kohli married Bollywood actress Anushka Sharma in December 2017. The couple welcomed their first child, a daughter named Vamika, in January 2021.
